  • Tristram Shandy is a nine-volume novel published between 1759 and 1767 by English novelist Laurence Sterne. The full title of the novel is The Life and Opinions of Tristram Shandy, Gentleman. This novel is basically a biography of a fictional character 'Tristram Shandy'. This novel contains many digressions which means the narrator of the novel often diverts from the main story and starts talking about something else.
  • Vol - 1 Tristram Shandy, the narrator of the novel decides to tell his life story from the very beginning, starting with the moment of Tristram's mother and father having sex and conceiving him. Tristram argues that the thoughts and feelings of the parents while having Sex to conceive a baby have a huge impact on the personality and fortunes of future child. In Tristram's case, his mother interrupted her husband while having Sex and this scattered and dispersed the sperm cells of Tristram's father which he believes could help in the growth and development of Tristram in the womb. In vol - 1 Tristram also introduces other character of the novel like his family, his father Walter Shandy, his mother Elizabeth Shandy, his uncle Toby Shandy, his house servants Susannah and Obadiah, his family doctor Dr.Slop and his elder brother Boby Shandy.
  • Vol - 2 Second volume explores a more detailed story of Uncle Toby, describing his war record and the wound in his groin area. The narration also flashes back to the day of Tristram's birth, when his father and uncle Toby were chatting in the living room. Finally, Susannah came to inform them, that Mrs. Shandy is about to deliver the child, Susannah calls the midwife, she arrives but for extra precaution Walter send Obadiah to call Dr. Slop. Dr. Slop came but the he did not bring his medicine bag, then Obadiah brings the bag as Walter asks him to do so.
  • Vol - 3 Before Dr. Slop can go upstairs and deliver the baby, he must open his doctor's bag, which has been bound up in complicated knots. Attempting to cut the knots with a knife, he cuts his thumb. After that, Susannah, the maidservant, runs downstairs to report Dr. Slop that things are not going well upstairs. Dr. Slop agrees to come up and assist, once he has tested out his forceps and his delivery technique on Uncle Toby's hands. While doing this, he cuts Toby's hand. After that, the doctor goes up to the bedchamber and while delivering the baby, Dr. Slop breaks Tristram’s nose and then he makes a new artificial nose.
  • Vol - 4 The 4th volume opens with "Slawkenbergius's Tale", a fictional character with a long nose. After this digression, the scene returns to Sandy's house where Walter was thinking the name of his newly born child. After that Susannah came to him and tell that the child may not survive and he needs to be baptized. Walter tells her that the child's name is 'Trismegistus', but Susannah forgets the name while going to the priest named Yorick and there she pronounced the name incorrectly as 'Tristram'. Later Walter asks church lawyer that if he can rename the child but the lawyer denied, and then they have to keep the name. Walter Shandy wants to give money to his elder son 'Boby' for his Europe tripbut then he receives news that Bobby is dead.
  • Vol - 5 Walter was very sad by Bobby’s death but then he became very conscious about Tristram’s education and he started writing Tristram-paedia in which he stated, how to teach his young child. Fast-forwarding to age five, Tristram describes a mishap in which he is circumcised (to cut off the skin of a man’s sexual organ) by a sliding window.
  • Vol - 6 In Vol - 6, Walter is concerned about Tristram’s education and he is looking for a tutor. He asks Toby, and Toby suggested Billy Le Fever, son of Lieutenant Le Fever. After that the narrator once again diverts from the main story and started telling about the death of Lieutenant Le Fever and the Vol - 6 ends.
  • Vol - 7 In the Vol - 7, Tristram is now in his 40s and his is preparing to travel to France in order to improve his health. Tristram travels to the different cities of France and he feels safe there. He did not talk much about his trip but only tell the reader how he feels there and also about his dance with a village girl, Nanette.
  • Vol - 8 Vol - 8 explores the love story of Uncle Toby and the Widow Wadman. The two first met when Toby moved up from London and shifted to Wadman's colony where he stayed in her guest room until his own house could be furnished. Mrs. Wadman always tried to flirt with Toby. Mrs. Wadman makes a advance upon Toby by pretending to have something in her eye and asking him to take a look. Struck by her beauty, he realizes he is in love with Mrs. Wadman and he decided to propose her.
  • Vol - 9 Toby goes to Mrs Wadman's house along with his servant Corporal Trim to propose her but he is scared about Wadman's reaction, then he summons his courage and walks to her house. Toby awkwardly confesses his love and proposes Mrs. Wadman for marriage. Mrs. Wadman, however, is mainly concerned about Toby's war wound, which she fears has left him impotent. Although Mrs. Wadman is already married but her husband could not satisfy her sexually and she is looking for a man who can satisfy her. After that Toby become upset when he come to know that she is marrying him just to fulfill her sexual desire and not because of love. Then, he goes to Shandy's house where Walter and Mrs. Shandy are chatting with Yorick and Dr. Slop and the novel closes with a lots of digressions and absurdity.
