  • SUMMARY:-
  • Here’s a summary of John Webster’s "The Duchess of Malfi" act by act:
  • Act 1 The play opens in the court of the Duke of Malfi. The Duchess, a young widow, lives with her two corrupt brothers: Ferdinand and the Cardinal. They are both highly protective and prohibit her from remarrying, fearing that a new marriage would diminish their control over her wealth. Despite this, the Duchess secretly falls in love with her steward, Antonio. Against her brothers' wishes, she privately marries Antonio, swearing him to secrecy. Bosola, a spy employed by Ferdinand, is placed in the Duchess's household to keep an eye on her actions.
  • Act 2 Time has passed, and the Duchess has had a child with Antonio, though they continue to keep their marriage a secret. Bosola grows suspicious of the Duchess when he notices her behavior during pregnancy. He eventually discovers the truth and sends a letter to Ferdinand informing him that the Duchess has given birth. Ferdinand, enraged by the news, begins plotting revenge against her.
  • Act 3 Ferdinand confronts the Duchess and accuses her of dishonoring their family by secretly marrying beneath her social rank. He threatens her, but she bravely defends her actions. The Duchess and Antonio decide to flee with their children, but Antonio is sent away for his safety. The Duchess, however, is captured by Ferdinand and Bosola. Ferdinand’s rage intensifies as he loses control over the situation.
  • Act 4 The Duchess is imprisoned in her own palace by Ferdinand, who subjects her to cruel psychological torture. He sends her wax figures of her dead husband and children, leading her to believe they have been murdered. Despite this torment, the Duchess remains strong and refuses to submit to her brothers' cruelty. Eventually, Ferdinand orders Bosola to strangle the Duchess. Bosola hesitates but ultimately follows through with the command. The Duchess is murdered, but her dignity and courage remain intact.
  • Act 5 Following the Duchess's death, Bosola regrets his actions and turns against Ferdinand and the Cardinal. Antonio, unaware of his wife's death, seeks reconciliation with the brothers. However, a series of tragic events ensue. The Cardinal murders his mistress, Julia, out of fear that she might expose his secrets. Bosola, seeking justice, accidentally kills Antonio in the process of trying to kill the Cardinal. In the final scene, Ferdinand, the Cardinal, and Bosola all die in a bloody confrontation, leaving the Duchess’s eldest son as the sole survivor to inherit the Malfi estate.
  • The play is a grim exploration of corruption, power, and the consequences of unchecked ambition. The Duchess’s tragic end is a result of the malicious and tyrannical forces that surround her, but her bravery and virtue remain intact throughout her suffering.
