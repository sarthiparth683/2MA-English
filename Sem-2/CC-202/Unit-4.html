• John Webster: The Duchess of Malfi

  • Written in 1612-13
  • Published in 1623

  • SUMMARY:-
  • Act 1 The play opens in the court of Malfi, where the recently widowed Duchess lives with her brothers, Ferdinand and the Cardinal. Ferdinand and the Cardinal are highly controlling and want to prevent their sister from remarrying to maintain control over her wealth. Despite their warnings, the Duchess secretly marries Antonio, her steward, and they have three children.
  • Act 2 The Duchess's secret marriage is soon discovered by her brothers. They hire Bosola, a malcontent and former convict, to spy on her. Bosola discovers the Duchess's pregnancy and informs Ferdinand.
  • Act 3 Ferdinand confronts the Duchess, revealing he knows about her children. He imprisons her and her children in her own palace. Bosola, initially complicit, begins to feel remorse for his actions.
  • Act 4 The Duchess is subjected to psychological torture by Ferdinand, who presents her with wax figures of her dead family to drive her to despair. Ultimately, Bosola is ordered to kill the Duchess and her children. He reluctantly complies, but his guilt grows.
  • Act 5 Antonio, unaware of his family's fate, attempts to reconcile with Ferdinand. Ferdinand, driven mad by guilt and lycanthropy, accidentally kills his own servant. Bosola, filled with remorse, seeks revenge on Ferdinand and the Cardinal. A series of misunderstandings and fatal encounters lead to the deaths of Antonio, the Cardinal, Ferdinand, and Bosola.
  • Themes: Power and Corruption: The play explores the corrupting influence of power, particularly in the actions of Ferdinand and the Cardinal. Fate and Free Will: The characters struggle with their destinies, often making choices that lead to their tragic ends. Class and Social Structure: The marriage between the Duchess and Antonio challenges the rigid social hierarchies of the time. "The Duchess of Malfi" is known for its dark themes, complex characters, and poetic language, making it a classic example of Jacobean tragedy.
