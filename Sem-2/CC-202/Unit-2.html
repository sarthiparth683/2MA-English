William Shakespeare: "Hamlet", and, "As You Like It".

  • 1. William Shakespeare: Hamlet

  • Full name of the Play is :- The tragedy of Hamlet Prince of Denmark
  • Longest play of William Shakespeare
  • Revenge and Tragedy Play
  • Performed in 1602
  • Published in 1603
  • Story Location:- Denmark
  • CHARACTERS:-
  • Hamlet (Prince of Denmark): Role: The tragic hero of the play, Hamlet is the Prince of Denmark, deeply affected by his father’s sudden death and his mother’s quick remarriage to his uncle Claudius. Characteristics: Hamlet is introspective, philosophical, and struggles with indecision. His intellect and wit make him reflective, yet his inability to act quickly causes internal conflict. He seeks revenge for his father's murder, but grapples with morality, life, death, and the consequences of his actions. His famous soliloquy “To be or not to be” reflects his existential dilemmas.
  • Claudius (King of Denmark): Role: Hamlet’s uncle and the antagonist of the play. Claudius has usurped the throne by killing Hamlet's father and marrying Queen Gertrude. Characteristics: Claudius is manipulative, ambitious, and deceitful. He is a skilled politician and ruler, but his guilt over his crime haunts him. Though capable of remorse, he ultimately prioritizes power over morality.
  • Gertrude (Queen of Denmark): Role: Hamlet’s mother and widow of King Hamlet, now married to Claudius. Characteristics: Gertrude is often seen as morally weak or passive. Her quick marriage to Claudius after her husband's death causes Hamlet great distress. While she loves her son, her decisions raise questions about her loyalty. She seems oblivious to Claudius’s treachery and often seeks peace and comfort over conflict.
  • Ghost of King Hamlet: Role: The spirit of Hamlet's deceased father who reveals the truth of his murder to Hamlet, urging him to seek revenge. Characteristics: The Ghost is solemn and commands Hamlet to avenge his death. He embodies the themes of uncertainty and the supernatural, and his appearance prompts Hamlet’s quest for justice.
  • Polonius (Chamberlain): Role: The chief counselor to King Claudius, and father to Ophelia and Laertes. Characteristics: Polonius is a verbose and meddling character, often offering advice full of clichés. He is somewhat self-important and obsessed with controlling others, particularly his children. His spying on Hamlet and meddling in royal affairs ultimately leads to his accidental death at Hamlet's hands.
  • Ophelia (Daughter of Polonius): Role: Polonius's daughter and Hamlet's love interest. Characteristics: Ophelia is portrayed as gentle, obedient, and vulnerable. She follows the orders of her father and brother, and her relationship with Hamlet is complicated by the power dynamics in the court. Hamlet's rejection and Polonius’s death drive her to madness, eventually leading to her tragic drowning.
  • Laertes (Son of Polonius): Role: Polonius's son, who seeks revenge on Hamlet for his father’s death. Characteristics: Laertes is passionate and impulsive, driven by loyalty to his family. Unlike Hamlet, he does not hesitate in his quest for vengeance. His fiery temper and willingness to be manipulated by Claudius contrast with Hamlet’s introspective nature, making him a foil to Hamlet.
  • Horatio (Loyal Friend to Hamlet): Role: Hamlet’s closest friend and confidante throughout the play. Characteristics: Horatio is level-headed, rational, and loyal. He serves as a grounding force for Hamlet and is one of the few characters Hamlet fully trusts. Horatio remains by Hamlet’s side until the end, tasked with telling Hamlet's story after his death.
  • Rosencrantz and Guildenstern (Friends of Hamlet): Role: Former school friends of Hamlet, who are summoned by Claudius to spy on Hamlet. Characteristics: Rosencrantz and Guildenstern are easily manipulated by Claudius and serve as pawns in the king's schemes. They appear friendly, but their betrayal of Hamlet leads to their eventual deaths. Their roles highlight themes of loyalty, friendship, and manipulation.
  • Fortinbras (Prince of Norway): Role: The Prince of Norway, seeking to avenge his father’s death, which was caused by Hamlet’s father. Characteristics: Fortinbras is decisive, courageous, and driven by a sense of honor. He is a parallel to Hamlet, as both seek revenge for their fathers. However, unlike Hamlet, Fortinbras acts with resolve. By the end of the play, Fortinbras arrives in Denmark and claims the throne, symbolizing a restoration of order.
  • SUMMARY:-
  • "Hamlet" by William Shakespeare is one of his most renowned tragedies, focusing on the complex character of Prince Hamlet of Denmark. Here's a summary of the play:
  • Act 1 The play opens with the appearance of the ghost of King Hamlet, the recently deceased King of Denmark. The ghost reveals to Prince Hamlet, his son, that he was murdered by his own brother Claudius, who has now taken the throne and married Hamlet's mother, Gertrude. The ghost demands that Hamlet avenge his murder.
  • Act 2 Hamlet begins to act erratically, raising concerns in the court about his mental state. Polonius, the chief advisor to the king, believes Hamlet's madness stems from his love for Polonius's daughter, Ophelia. Claudius and Gertrude try to understand the cause of Hamlet's behavior, enlisting the help of Rosencrantz and Guildenstern, Hamlet’s old friends.
  • Act 3 Hamlet stages a play, "The Mousetrap," which mirrors the events of his father’s murder, to gauge Claudius’s guilt. Claudius’s reaction to the play confirms his guilt to Hamlet. Gertrude confronts Hamlet about his behavior, leading to a heated argument during which Hamlet accidentally kills Polonius, thinking he is Claudius spying on them.
  • Act 4 Claudius, fearing for his life, sends Hamlet to England with Rosencrantz and Guildenstern, carrying orders for Hamlet to be executed. Meanwhile, Ophelia, distraught over her father’s death, drowns. Her brother Laertes returns to Denmark, seeking revenge for Polonius's death. Claudius manipulates Laertes into a plot to kill Hamlet using a poisoned sword.
  • Act 5 Hamlet returns to Denmark after escaping the execution plot. He confronts Laertes at Ophelia’s funeral, and they grapple in her grave. In the final scene, a fencing match between Hamlet and Laertes is arranged. Both are wounded by the poisoned sword, and Gertrude accidentally drinks poisoned wine intended for Hamlet. Laertes, dying, reveals Claudius’s plot. Hamlet kills Claudius before succumbing to the poison himself. As the royal family lies dead, Fortinbras of Norway arrives to find the Danish court in chaos and claims the throne.
  • Themes "Hamlet" explores themes such as revenge, madness, mortality, and the complexity of action. Hamlet's internal struggles and philosophical musings on life and death are central to the play, making it a rich text for analysis and interpretation.

  • 2. William Shakespeare: As You Like It.

  • Place - Forest of Ardenne
  • Written in 1599
  • Published in 1623
  • CHARACTERS:-
  • 1. Rosalind (Ganymede) Role: The protagonist and heroine of the play, Rosalind is Duke Senior's daughter, who has been exiled. When her uncle, Duke Frederick, banishes her from the court, she disguises herself as a young man named Ganymede and flees to the Forest of Arden. Characteristics: Rosalind is witty, intelligent, and resourceful. She is known for her sharp sense of humor and keen insight into human behavior. In her disguise as Ganymede, she navigates complex relationships, including her love for Orlando. Her transformation into Ganymede allows her to explore gender roles and challenge societal expectations.
  • 2. Celia (Aliena) Role: Celia is Rosalind's cousin and the daughter of Duke Frederick. When Rosalind is banished, Celia chooses to leave the court and accompany her to the Forest of Arden. Celia takes on the alias Aliena in the forest. Characteristics: Celia is loyal, kind-hearted, and devoted to Rosalind. Despite being more reserved than Rosalind, she shows great courage and friendship. She has a deep bond with Rosalind, and her decision to leave her privileged life behind demonstrates her love for her cousin.
  • 3. Touchstone Role: The court jester who accompanies Rosalind and Celia to the Forest of Arden. He provides comic relief in the play with his wit and absurdity, while also engaging in philosophical conversations with other characters. Characteristics: Touchstone is a clown, but his humor often reveals deeper truths about life and society. He is clever, sarcastic, and enjoys pointing out the follies of others. Despite his crude jokes, he is insightful in his observations of human nature.
  • 4. Orlando Role: The romantic hero of the play and the younger brother of Oliver. Orlando falls in love with Rosalind at first sight and later wins a wrestling match against the court’s wrestler, Charles. He escapes to the Forest of Arden when he fears for his life. Characteristics: Orlando is brave, loyal, and virtuous, though he is somewhat naive in matters of love. He is portrayed as noble and strong, yet he lacks confidence early in the play, especially in his strained relationship with his brother, Oliver. His love for Rosalind drives much of the plot's action.
  • 5. Oliver Role: Orlando’s older brother, who initially treats Orlando cruelly and with envy. Oliver conspires to have Orlando killed but later experiences a change of heart when Orlando saves his life in the forest. Characteristics: At first, Oliver is envious, manipulative, and antagonistic toward Orlando. He withholds Orlando’s inheritance and even plots his brother's death. However, after being saved by Orlando, Oliver undergoes a significant transformation, becoming kind and remorseful.
  • 6. Duke Senior Role: Rosalind’s father and the rightful Duke of the court. He has been exiled by his younger brother, Duke Frederick, and lives in the Forest of Arden with his loyal followers. Characteristics: Duke Senior is depicted as wise, kind, and philosophical. Even in exile, he maintains a positive outlook on life, finding solace in the simplicity and freedom of the forest. He is a benevolent leader and a loving father to Rosalind.
  • 7. Duke Frederick Role: The villain of the play, Duke Frederick is the younger brother of Duke Senior. He usurps Duke Senior’s position, exiling him and taking over his dukedom. He also banishes Rosalind, despite her being his niece. Characteristics: Duke Frederick is power-hungry, tyrannical, and ruthless. He is driven by jealousy and ambition, showing little regard for familial bonds. His harsh and arbitrary actions set much of the plot in motion. However, like many characters in the play, he too undergoes a change, repenting and renouncing his wrongdoings by the end of the play.
  • 8. Charles Role: The court wrestler who is loyal to Duke Frederick. He is manipulated by Oliver to harm Orlando during their wrestling match, but Orlando ultimately defeats him. Characteristics: Charles is strong, competitive, and somewhat prideful. Though his role is small, he is portrayed as a loyal servant to Duke Frederick and is used by Oliver in his plot against Orlando.
  • SUMMARY:-
  • "As You Like It" by William Shakespeare is a pastoral comedy that explores themes of love, identity, and the natural world. Here is a summary of the play, act by act:
  • Act 1: Scene 1: Orlando, a young nobleman, laments his poor treatment by his older brother, Oliver. He decides to challenge Duke Frederick's champion wrestler, Charles. Scene 2: Rosalind, the daughter of the banished Duke Senior, and her cousin Celia, the daughter of Duke Frederick, watch the wrestling match. Orlando wins, and he and Rosalind fall in love at first sight. Scene 3: Duke Frederick banishes Rosalind from the court. Celia decides to join her cousin in exile. They disguise themselves: Rosalind as a young man named Ganymede, and Celia as his sister, Aliena. They plan to seek refuge in the Forest of Arden.
  • Act 2: Scene 1: Duke Senior and his followers live a contented life in the Forest of Arden. Scene 2: Duke Frederick orders the capture of Rosalind and Celia, believing they have fled with Orlando. Scene 3: Orlando, warned by his faithful servant Adam, flees to the Forest of Arden to escape his brother's plots against him. Scene 4: Rosalind, Celia, and their clown, Touchstone, arrive in the Forest of Arden, tired and hungry. Scene 5: The melancholy Jaques philosophizes on life while Duke Senior's men sing a cheerful song. Scene 6: Orlando and Adam, exhausted from their journey, find rest and sustenance in the forest.
  • Act 3: Scene 1: Duke Frederick commands Oliver to find Orlando, threatening to confiscate his property. Scene 2: Orlando, now in the forest, pins love poems to Rosalind on trees. Rosalind (as Ganymede) reads them and decides to test Orlando's love by pretending to be his beloved Rosalind. Scene 3: Touchstone flirts with a country girl named Audrey. Jaques warns them about the foolishness of their plans to marry. Scene 4: Rosalind, Celia, and Corin (a shepherd) discuss Orlando's tardiness in meeting Ganymede (Rosalind's disguise). Scene 5: Phoebe, a shepherdess, falls in love with Ganymede, who rebukes her harshly.
  • Act 4: Scene 1: Ganymede continues to test Orlando's love. They playact a mock marriage ceremony. Scene 2: Jaques and the foresters celebrate the successful hunt of a deer. Scene 3: Oliver, having been saved by Orlando from a lioness, arrives in the forest. He repents for his past cruelty and tells Ganymede and Aliena about Orlando's bravery.
  • Act 5: Scene 1: Touchstone and Audrey plan their wedding. Scene 2: Oliver and Celia (Aliena) fall in love and decide to marry. Ganymede promises to resolve all romantic entanglements the next day. Scene 3: Touchstone mocks a couple of pages singing a romantic song. Scene 4: Ganymede reveals her true identity as Rosalind. Duke Senior is reunited with his daughter.
  • Several couples are happily paired off: Orlando and Rosalind, Oliver and Celia, Touchstone and Audrey, and Silvius and Phoebe. News arrives that Duke Frederick has repented and restored Duke Senior to his rightful place.
  • Epilogue: Rosalind steps forward to deliver a witty and charming epilogue, addressing the audience directly and inviting them to enjoy the play's happy resolution. "As You Like It" is celebrated for its exploration of love in its various forms, its memorable characters, and its delightful blending of the comedic and the pastoral.
