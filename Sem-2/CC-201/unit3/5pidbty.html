• 3.2-G.M. Hopkins:Pied Beauty

  • Summary:- Celebration of Variety and God's Creation Theme: The poem praises God for the beauty found in variety and the intricate details of the natural world. Structure: "Pied Beauty" is a shortened sonnet (curtal sonnet) with a unique rhyme scheme (ABC ABC).
  • The poem opens with the word "Glory" and an appreciation for "dappled things" - objects with contrasting colors or markings. The speaker lists various examples: the "brightness" of the sky contrasted with "lawn-byes" (open spaces between woods), the "brinded" (streaked) cow, the "finnches' wings" with their colorful patterns. Even imperfections like freckles and speckled trout become objects of praise. The poem highlights the beauty of man-made objects too, like "willow-wrenches" (tools) and "counter," the grain counting tool used by farmers. The final line, "fathers forth as handiwork," emphasizes God's role as the creator who brings forth this diverse and beautiful world.
  • Key Points Focus on Details: Hopkins doesn't just praise generic beauty; he finds wonder in the specific details - speckles, freckles, dappling. All Creation Matters: From the grand sky to the humble tools, everything is part of God's creation and worthy of praise. Awe and Wonder: The tone is one of awe and wonder at the richness and complexity of the world. Overall, "Pied Beauty" is a celebration of God's artistry in creating a world brimming with variety and exquisite detail.
