• 2.1 Matthew Arnold:- Dover Beach

  • Dramatic Monologue
  • Written in 1851
  • Published in 1867
  • Dover Beach is present in England but ( lies between England and France )
  • Main themes are nature,loss of Christianity and love.
  • Summary:-
  • Stanza 1 The speaker describes the beautiful and tranquil scene of the moonlit sea at Dover Beach, looking out over the English Channel. The calmness and peacefulness of the setting are highlighted. However, the sound of the waves brings a sense of melancholy, suggesting that beneath the surface beauty, there is something deeper and sadder.
  • Stanza 2 The speaker shifts to ancient Greece, recalling how the Greek playwright Sophocles also heard the sound of waves, which made him think of human misery. The eternal ebb and flow of the sea becomes a metaphor for human suffering, connecting the speaker's present feelings with the universal human condition across time.
  • Stanza 3 The speaker laments the loss of faith in the modern world. He describes the “Sea of Faith” that once surrounded the world, providing comfort and certainty. But now, this faith is receding, leaving the world bare and exposed to uncertainty and confusion, just like a beach stripped of its waters.
  • Stanza 4 In the final stanza, the speaker turns to his companion and pleads for love and loyalty, as these are the only sources of comfort in a world filled with uncertainty and despair. He portrays the world as a place of chaos and struggle, where appearances can be deceptive, and where truth and joy are hard to find.
  • Overall Theme: The poem reflects on the decline of religious faith and the resulting feelings of uncertainty and isolation in the modern world. It emphasizes the need for love and human connection amidst the changing tides of existence.
