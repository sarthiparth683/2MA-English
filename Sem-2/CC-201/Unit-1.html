  • 1. William Wordsworth: Michael

  • written in :-1798
  • Published in :- 1800
  • it is a Lyrical Ballad (more focused on storytelling, lyrical ballads explore personal feelings, thoughts, and emotions.)
  • Characters:-
  • Michael: all family members are shepards,and they live in cottage called Evening Star, the protagonist of the poem. Michael is eighty years old at the start of the poem.
  • Isabel: Wife of Michael, she is a prodigious(great or impressive) woman. She spends her time spinning wool and flax, and is the mother of Luke. She is sixty years old at the start of the poem.
  • Luke: The son and only child of Michael and Isabel. He is eighteen years old at the start of the poem.
  • Summary:-
  • "Michael: A Pastoral Poem" is a narrative poem by William Wordsworth, written in 1798 and published in the 1800 edition of "Lyrical Ballads." The poem is one of Wordsworth's most famous works, focusing on rural life and the emotional struggles of ordinary people.
  • Plot: Michael, a hardworking and honest shepherd, has spent his entire life tending to his sheep on his farm, known as "The Evening Star." He is deeply connected to the land and the traditions of his ancestors. Michael's love for his family and his land is profound, and he takes great pride in his work.
  • As Michael and Isabel grow older, they face financial difficulties, primarily due to a debt Michael had guaranteed for a relative. To save the farm from being lost, Michael decides to send their son, Luke, to work in the city. Although this decision is heartbreaking for the family, Michael believes it is necessary for their survival.
  • Before Luke departs, Michael takes him to a special spot on their land and shows him an unfinished sheepfold (a stone enclosure for sheep) that they were building together. Michael asks Luke to finish the work when he returns, symbolizing the hope that the son will come back to carry on the family's legacy.
  • However, Luke is seduced by the vices of city life and never returns home. The poem ends tragically, with Michael and Isabel dying, and the farm being lost. The unfinished sheepfold remains as a poignant reminder of the family's broken dreams and the loss of their way of life.
  • Themes: Rural Life and Tradition: The poem emphasizes the connection between people and the land, highlighting the simplicity, beauty, and hardships of rural life. Loss and Change: The poem explores the pain of loss, both personal and cultural, as the traditional way of life is threatened by external forces like economic pressures and urbanization. Family and Legacy: Michael's deep love for his son and his desire to pass on the family farm to Luke are central to the poem's emotional impact.

  • Summary:-
  • In the unfinished gothic poem(Horror theme) Christabel by S.T. Coleridge, a late night encounter in the woods sets the stage for a chilling mystery. The Spark of Intrigue: The poem opens on a dark April night with Sir Leoline's loyal dog howling, foreshadowing trouble. Christabel, Sir Leoline's innocent daughter, ventures into the woods to pray for her betrothed. There, she discovers Geraldine, a beautiful woman claiming to be abducted by bandits. Taking Refuge, Finding Fear: Filled with compassion, Christabel brings Geraldine back to the castle. As they prepare for bed, Christabel notices a strange mark on Geraldine's breast, but a mysterious spell prevents her from speaking of it. Geraldine's unsettling behavior, including a cold touch and mesmerizing eyes, arouses Christabel's suspicion. A Father's Trust, a Daughter's Doubt: Sir Leoline warmly welcomes Geraldine, creating tension between him and Christabel. Christabel tries to warn her father about Geraldine's odd behavior, but the spell renders her helpless. Leoline, misinterpreting Christabel's unease as jealousy, becomes angry and distrustful of his own daughter. The Unresolved Mystery: The poem ends with Sir Leoline turning his back on Christabel and embracing Geraldine. The unresolved narrative leaves us hanging: Will Christabel break free from the spell? Will Sir Leoline discover Geraldine's true nature? Themes at Play: The poem explores the classic battle between good and evil, with Christabel representing purity and Geraldine embodying darkness. It delves into the deceptive nature of appearances, where beauty can mask sinister intentions. The power dynamics between the female characters shift as Christabel becomes controlled by Geraldine. Christabel, though unfinished, remains a captivating work for its suspenseful atmosphere, its gothic elements, and its exploration of timeless themes.
