  • 1. William Wordsworth: Michael

  • written in :-1798
  • Published in :- 1800
  • it is a Lyrical Ballad (more focused on storytelling, lyrical ballads explore personal feelings, thoughts, and emotions.)
  • Characters:-
  • Michael: all family members are shepards,and they live in cottage called Evening Star, the protagonist of the poem. Michael is eighty years old at the start of the poem.
  • Isabel: Wife of Michael, she is a prodigious(great or impressive) woman. She spends her time spinning wool and flax, and is the mother of Luke. She is sixty years old at the start of the poem.
  • Luke: The son and only child of Michael and Isabel. He is eighteen years old at the start of the poem.
  • Summary:-
  • "Michael: A Pastoral Poem" by William Wordsworth tells the tragic story of Michael, an old shepherd, and his son Luke. The poem reflects Wordsworth's emphasis on nature, rural life, and human emotion. Here's a stanza-wise summary:
  • Stanzas 1-3 Wordsworth introduces the setting of the poem, a remote valley where Michael, a shepherd, has lived his entire life. The poet describes the serene and beautiful landscape, emphasizing the simplicity and peace of rural life. The valley, known as "Green-head Ghyll," becomes a symbol of nature's enduring presence.
  • Stanzas 4-5 The poem introduces Michael, an old shepherd who is deeply connected to the land. He lives with his wife, Isabel, and their only son, Luke. Michael is described as a man of integrity, strength, and emotional depth, who loves his land and family. Despite his age, he is still strong and hardworking.
  • Stanzas 6-9 Michael's life is tied to his land, and he takes pride in his work as a shepherd. Wordsworth describes the bond between Michael and Luke, a young boy who has grown up under his father's guidance. Michael dreams of leaving his land and legacy to Luke, teaching him the values of hard work, honesty, and a love of nature.
  • Stanzas 10-13 However, Michael faces financial difficulties. To save his farm, he is forced to send Luke to the city to work for a merchant relative. Michael's decision is filled with sorrow, but he hopes that Luke will return to the valley to continue the family legacy. Before Luke leaves, Michael takes him to a spot on the farm, where they begin to build a sheepfold together—a symbolic gesture of the future they both hope for.
  • Stanzas 14-17 Luke leaves for the city, and Michael and Isabel are left alone. The separation is painful, but they remain hopeful that Luke will succeed and return. Michael works on the sheepfold, but as time passes, news from Luke becomes less frequent.
  • Stanzas 18-20 Tragically, Luke succumbs to the temptations of city life and loses his moral compass. He commits a crime and brings shame to himself and his family. Michael is devastated by the news. The poem describes how Michael continues to work on the sheepfold, but it remains unfinished, a symbol of his broken hopes and dreams.
  • Stanzas 21-24 In the final stanzas, Michael’s spirit is crushed by the loss of Luke. His deep connection to the land and his family’s legacy is forever altered. Wordsworth emphasizes the sorrow and grief that accompany Michael's situation. Michael dies with his dream unfulfilled, and the sheepfold remains incomplete—a symbol of the family’s disintegration.
  • Conclusion The poem reflects Wordsworth's themes of nature, human emotion, and the struggles of rural life. Michael’s story is one of love, loss, and the passage of time, with nature remaining a constant presence amid human suffering.
  • This stanza-wise breakdown captures the essence of each part of the poem, showcasing the emotional depth and pastoral imagery characteristic of Wordsworth’s work.
